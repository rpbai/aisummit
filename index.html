What is AI?

 
When we talk about Artificial Intelligence, does that mean we are talking about a machine with human-like intelligence?
Well yes. That’s what it is!
Artificial Intelligence is the technology that enables a machine to work intelligently and display its ability to learn as well as solve problems on its own.
So, we can say that when a machine can mimic human intelligence, it is termed as artificially intelligent. In other words, an AI machine has human-like abilities to solve real-world problems, improvise based on its learnings and experiences, make predictions according to the information it has and make decisions on its own.

How do machines learn?

After exploring the abilities of Artificial Intelligence, it is now time for us to see how does this happen.
Let us understand this in two simple steps.
Similar to us, machines too learn from training and experiences.
Remember the first time you were taught mathematics?
  • Starting with numbers, you went through basic arithmetic operations and now you can easily calculate and maintain finances in your daily lives.
  • Information that was taught to us helped us understand the concepts and our experiences improved our performance.
Same is the case with AI too.
Machines are fed with data, which is the information used to build the foundation of their intelligence.
  • Machines are trained on this data and they develop their own understanding around it in the form of algorithms.
  • Once the machine starts functioning, it keeps on updating its algorithm according to its own experiences.
  • Just like us, machines also gather feedback on the mistakes they have made and improvise accordingly.
Here’s how this actually happens:
  1. The machine collects data - Data Acquisition
  2. It analyses the data to find patterns – Data Exploration
  3. It tries out different solutions- Solution Modeling
  4. Receives feedback and figures out the best solution - Evaluation
  5. It implements the optimal solution – Deployment

Domains ( CV - Introduction)

  • As the name suggests, Computer Vision is the science of making machines see.
  • Applications of AI that fall under this domain work with visual data like images and videos.
  • There are a lot of CV based technologies that are not actually powered by AI, like your digital camera or digital TV screens!
  • At the same time, we have many technologies powered by AI that use CV, like your Instagram filters or object detection in self-driving cars!

Let us understand CV in two small steps.
  • Humans perceive sight in the form of colors and shapes.
  • Have you ever heard of pixels and resolutions?
  • Take a look at the picture of the rose below. If we try zooming into the petals, we will soon be seeing blurred square boxes. Each blurred square box is a pixel.
  • Every pixel is a set of numbers indicating the colors that we see.
  • CV is essentially the technique that the computers use to process the set of numbers that are associated with pixels.
  • CV acts as a link between AI and the image or video data.
The wide range of AI applications under this domain are:
  • Security systems (face detection),
  • On-screen fingerprint sensing
  • Creating new artwork by combining images and more.
